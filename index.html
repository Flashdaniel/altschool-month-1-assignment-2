Biography

Daniel Nweze was born on November 5th, 1996, in Enugu, Nigeria, to Alphonse and Florence Nweze. He is the first of seven children, two of whom were adopted into the family.

Growing up, Daniel faced significant challenges at home. His father, Alphonse Nweze, was an abusive, unsupportive man who struggled with a womanizing nature. His disregard for his family, particularly his children, left a deep impact on Daniel. Despite being their biological father, Alphonse often acted as though he wasn’t involved in their lives, which created a difficult and emotionally strained environment for the children.

On the other hand, Daniel’s mother, Florence Nweze, is a loving and devoted parent. She worked tirelessly to provide for her children, always going above and beyond to ensure their well-being and happiness. Her unwavering love and support made her the pillar of strength in the family, and she is cherished by all of her children.

Daniel has always been a curious individual with a passion for learning. His interests span across various areas, but he has a particular affinity for building electrical devices and exploring technology. This inquisitiveness and drive for knowledge have shaped his current career aspirations.

Currently, Daniel is pursuing a career in the tech industry. His ambition is to be among the top 3% most successful software engineers in the world. Although he has been learning on his own, he is excited to have recently gained admission to Altschool Africa, where he will continue to further his education and skills in the tech field.

Through perseverance, determination, and the support of his mother, Daniel is determined to make a name for himself in the world of technology, overcoming his challenging upbringing and reaching for success.
